6.1 Blog: Financial Technology

URL: /blogs/financial-technology-rewriting-modern-finance
How Financial Technology Is Rewriting the Rules of Modern Finance
Author: Savitur Editorial Team  |  Date: 12 March 2026  |  Read time: 8 min  |  Categories: FinTech · Strategy
/images/static/banners/process-digitisation-web-banner.jpg
(Cover image)
From telegraph-era wire transfers to AI-driven robo-advisors, fintech has become the connective tissue of modern finance. Here's a clear-eyed look at what changed, what's next, and what it means for finance leaders today.

The quiet revolution in financial services

Financial technology — fintech, in shorthand — has gone from buzzword to backbone. What began as scattered experiments in digital payments and online trading is now the operating layer for almost every meaningful interaction between people, businesses, and money. Whether you are a CFO managing global treasury flows, a fund manager overseeing investor reporting, or a founder taking your first business banking steps, the platforms you depend on are fintech platforms — even if they don't advertise themselves that way.

The roots run far deeper than most people assume. The transatlantic telegraph cable of the late 1800s arguably kicked off the first wave of "fintech" by collapsing the time it took for financial information to cross continents. The U.S. Federal Reserve's launch of Fedwire in 1918 — moving funds between member banks over telegraph lines — was, in essence, the first electronic payments network. Diners Club's universal credit card in 1950, the world's first ATM in London in 1967, the founding of NASDAQ as the first electronic stock exchange in 1971, and the establishment of SWIFT in 1973: each of these was, at the time, a radical fintech moment.

Why the last fifteen years are different

What has changed since the late 2000s is the speed and depth of the disruption. Three forces converged. First, the 2008 financial crisis dented public trust in incumbent institutions and created daylight for technology-led alternatives. Second, the smartphone became near-universal, putting a programmable financial endpoint in every consumer's pocket. Third, the cloud collapsed the cost of building and scaling new financial products from millions of dollars and many years to thousands of dollars and a few months.

Eight sectors every finance leader should understand

  1. Banking and personal finance — neobanks, expense tracking, integrated financial planning
  2. Payments and remittances — peer-to-peer, cross-border, B2B and merchant acceptance
  3. Lending and credit — peer-to-peer, buy-now-pay-later, ML-driven underwriting for thin-file borrowers
  4. Investment and wealth management — robo-advisory, fractional investing, alternative asset platforms
  5. Capital markets technology — algorithmic trading, market data, execution analytics
  6. Insurtech — telematics-driven motor insurance, parametric covers, digital claims

Open banking and the API economy

The deeper structural shift, often invisible to end users, is the standardisation of APIs across the financial system. What this means for Indian businesses

India is one of the world's most active fintech markets. UPI alone now processes more transactions than several global card networks combined. The account aggregator framework, e-invoicing under GST, and TReDS for receivables financing have created a uniquely connected financial stack for businesses of every size.

Where the puck is moving

Three trends will define the next five years: embedded finance, AI-native financial workflows, and tokenisation of real-world assets.

6.3 Blog: Financial Audit

URL: /blogs/inside-modern-financial-audit
Inside the Modern Financial Audit: A Practitioner's Guide
Author: Savitur Editorial Team  |  Date: 14 February 2026  |  Read time: 10 min  |  Categories: Audit · Compliance
/images/static/banners/business-consulting-web-banner.jpg
Audit isn't a once-a-year ritual — it's a strategic check on the integrity of your financial reporting. We unpack the four phases, the standards, the opinions, and how technology is reshaping the auditor's toolkit.

What an audit actually is

A financial audit is an independent, evidence-driven examination of an organisation's financial statements, performed by qualified auditors against defined professional standards, ending in a formal opinion on whether those statements are presented fairly.

The four phases of an audit engagement

  1. Phase 1: Planning and risk assessment — understand the business, identify higher-risk areas, set materiality thresholds, assess internal controls.
  2. Phase 2: Tests of controls and transactions — test whether controls operate as intended; sample and trace entries through to source documents.
  3. Phase 3: Substantive procedures and balance testing — bank confirmations, inventory observations, analytical procedures.
  4. Phase 4: Conclusion and reporting — form opinion, issue audit report and management letters.

The four kinds of audit opinion

  • Unqualified (clean) opinion — fairly presented in all material respects.
  • Qualified opinion — fairly presented, except for specific issues.
  • Adverse opinion — materially misleading on the whole.
  • Disclaimer of opinion — insufficient evidence to form an opinion.

The big shift: technology in the audit

Data analytics tools now allow auditors to test 100% of journal entries. Machine learning models flag unusual patterns. The implication: audits can be deeper without being more disruptive — provided the company's data is clean and well structured.

GST, TDS and Tax Compliance: A No-Nonsense Guide for Founders
Author: Savitur Editorial Team  |  Date: 22 December 2025  |  Read time: 8 min  |  Categories: Taxation · Compliance
/images/static/banners/business-consulting-web-banner.jpg
Tax compliance is where most early-stage businesses quietly leak money and credibility. A founder-friendly walkthrough of GST, TDS, and the operating habits that keep you out of trouble — and out of penalty.

GST in a paragraph

India's Goods and Services Tax replaced a tangled web of central and state indirect taxes in July 2017. Registration is mandatory above prescribed turnover thresholds (₹20 lakh or ₹40 lakh, varying by state and supply type). The day-to-day rhythm involves issuing tax invoices, filing GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B, claiming input tax credit, and reconciling with GSTR-2B every month.

The five GST mistakes we see most often

  • Wrong HSN or rate classification
  • Claiming ITC against invoices that don't appear in GSTR-2B
  • Missed reverse-charge accounting
  • Late return filing
  • Inadequate documentation

TDS in a paragraph

Tax Deducted at Source applies to salaries, contractor/professional fees, rent, interest, commissions, dividends, and payments to non-residents. Deduct at the right rate when the payment is made or credited, deposit by the 7th of the following month, and file quarterly returns (Forms 24Q, 26Q, 27Q, 27EQ).

Building the operating discipline

Calendar everything · Reconcile monthly (books to GSTR-2B, payroll to TDS deposits) · Keep a clean documentation trail · Engage your CA early and often.
